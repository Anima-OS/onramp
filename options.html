Triage, QA & More

A great product is made of a lot more than code.

QA

You can't ship a product that matters without Quality Assurance, and every bug the QA team catches is one our users don't. If you want to get started testing pre-release versions of Firefox, sign up for One And Done, the entry point to the QA team's community program.

Triage All The Things.

Triage is the most important part of a bug's lifecycle, and Mozilla sees a lot of new bugs filed every day. If you can give us a few minutes of your day, you can help Mozilla move a lot faster by sorting a few bugs into the right categories so they get to the right people.

You can sign up to the TriageBot right here.

Improve our harnesses, tools, and tests.

Mozilla has dozens of harnesses and tools which need constant updating and cleanup. Most of this is due to new features, platforms, or new use cases. A simple way to start is looking at Good First Bugs or by getting involved in a larger project such as the Quarter of Contribution, Outreachy(we try to have projects each cycle), or Google Summer of Code.

Follow us on Twitter

We have a lot of twitter feeds at Mozilla, but a few good places to start are @mozilla, the Firefox account, and Start Mozilla, for Good First Bugs and other ways to join in.

Thank you

Mozilla's strength is the community behind it; Firefox is the product of a global development team working to keep the Web free, open and participatory, and your contributions will make Firefox and the Web better for hundreds of millions of people around the world.
